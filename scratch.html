Press the Spacebar

Description

The goal of the game is to have the cat sprite survive as long as possible by jumping over
the basketballs. As you progress through the game the basketballs will roll down the hill faster.
The score is increased by 1 for each jump over a basketball. The game ends when the cat sprite
makes contact with a basketball.

Reflection Questions

Describe some programming concepts or structures you learned in this unit that you applied in your game.

I learned that there are several ways to do specific things. For example, the scoring system can count in
two ways. The first way is to make the score go up every certain amount of seconds. The second way is to make
the score go up whenever something, the ball in this case, passes a certain area.

Did you encounter any problems or struggles while working on this project? If you did, how did you solve
or overcome them?

Yes, we, Chandler and I, encountered several problems. One of them was how to make the ball go faster. We
solved this by setting a high score that u can't exceed, and we controlled every level from 1-25. Another
problem that we encountered was time because we weren't sure what to do until we started designing the actual
game in scratch.

What aspects of your game do you like best?

I like the scoring system because I think that it's smart that the score goes up by 1 when the basketball
passes a certain point

If more time was given for you to work on your game, what would you improve or modify about your game?

I would add a "Gameover" screen and a better winning screen.
